## User Challenges — wedge & positioning (8)

### 5. Pick ONE lead wedge for the hero
- **What:** Pick lead pitch (recommended: "AI agents that hold up in production, with the eval suite that proves it"). Demote enablement and OSS to proof points.
- **Why:** CEO + Design both flagged — three pitches in the hero dilute each other; buyer reads in <10s and sees a Venn diagram, not a wedge
- **Effort:** M — copy rewrite of 3.1, 3.7
- **Priority:** P2

### 6. Differentiator #1 (eval suite) — rewrite as named methodology
- **What:** Replace "eval suite ships with the agent" with named methodology + measurement standard (e.g., "the avad.ai trajectory-coverage rubric: published, versioned, benchmarked against [N] public agent traces")
- **Why:** LangSmith, Braintrust, Patronus, OpenAI Evals, Anthropic eval cookbook, Inspect, Promptfoo all ship eval tooling — this is table stakes. Methodology with a name + benchmark IS defensible
- **Effort:** M — requires the rubric to actually exist publicly
- **Priority:** P2

### 7. Differentiator #2 (model-agnostic) — soften to "model-portable with measured drift"
- **What:** Replace "Model-agnostic by contract" with "model-portable with measured drift" + a regression-budget number
- **Why:** MCP/A2A reduce integration friction, not behavioral portability. Sophisticated buyers catch the overpromise
- **Effort:** S — copy edit + add a real drift number
- **Priority:** P2

### 8. Differentiator #3 (capability transfers) — put the rubric on the page
- **What:** Move the published capability-transfer rubric ON the page with a sample row (e.g., "Engineer can author a new trajectory eval test → before: 3/10, after: 8/10")
- **Why:** Every consultancy claims "capability transfer." The published rubric is the only real differentiator and is currently buried
- **Effort:** S — copy + sample row table
- **Priority:** P2 — depends on rubric being publishable

### 9. Reframe competitive set to Big 4 + boutiques
- **What:** In Section 1 "Competitive landscape" research brief, replace Scale AI / Cohere / Aleph Alpha / Mistral framing with Accenture AI practice / Deloitte AI Institute / BCG Gamma+X / McKinsey QuantumBlack / IBM Consulting / Capgemini / Thoughtworks plus boutiques (Cognition Labs services, Anthropic professional services, LangChain consulting)
- **Why:** Foundation-model + sovereign-AI companies aren't the procurement enemy. Real buyer has Big 4 in their RFP shortlist
- **Effort:** S — research brief rewrite
- **Priority:** P2

### 10. Soften "not on critical path after week 12" + add post-handover offer
- **What:** Soften to "your team can operate it by week 12; we are available for extension on terms you choose." Add a post-handover Eval-as-a-Service / quarterly eval refresh offer.
- **Why:** Many enterprise buyers want a 24-month partner. Disqualifying yourself from the largest contracts; LTV math suffers.
- **Effort:** M — copy + actually building the post-handover offer (productization)
- **Priority:** P2 — depends on whether firm wants to productize

### 11. Pricing model — add procurement-friendly secondary structure
- **What:** Add to FAQ: published blended rate for vendor-onboarding compliance, milestone billing against fixed-fee SOW, change-order mechanics, liability caps. Keep "fixed scope" primary.
- **Why:** Most F1000 vendor onboarding requires hourly rate disclosure even if the contract is fixed-scope. "We do not bill by the hour for senior work" fails procurement onboarding
- **Effort:** M — needs procurement / finance / legal alignment
- **Priority:** P2

### 12. Use `avadx` as canonical dev namespace
- **What:** Pick `avadx` as canonical dev namespace. Use `@avadx/*` for npm, `avadx-*` for PyPI, `github.com/avadx/*` for repos, `avadx` CLI binary. Keep `avad.ai` as firm/website brand only. Document at avadx org root.
- **Why:** DX flagged — three plausible spellings (avad.ai, avadx, avadai) breaks naming guessability
- **Effort:** XS — naming convention decision + AGENTS.md at org root
- **Priority:** P1 — gates section 3.9
